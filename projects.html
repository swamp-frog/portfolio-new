"On the Balcony of Orange Street"

This two part book is my attempt at telling the story of the lost and found photographs of a dutch engineer in Suriname. The book invites the readers to take in the period scenery before slowly revealing the village where it all took place. Originally captured in the 1900s, the photographs have been collected from the Rijks Museum archive and repurposed into this book.

publication, 2024
"Onion Creek, Austin, Texas"

If you moved to Onion Creek, Austin, Texas, today, and lived there for the rest of your life, would you be certain to say, on your death bed, that your neighbour 5 streets down changed his bathroom sink in 2019? If you owned this book, you would. Using very specific data from the city of Austin, Texas, this book is a collection of entries made to give readers a unique and personal view of a neighbourhood in the United States.

publication, 2025
"Poetry Magazine"

A printed adaptation of the online Poetry Magazine issue of April 2024. Designed using additional layers of see through paper, they allow the viewer to read multiple spreads at the same time. The layout combines original poems with their translated versions and imagery.

publication, 2025
"Without a Trace"

A revival of the original and out of print "How to disappear in China without a trace" by Susanne Bürner. This adaptation puts a spin on the function of the book. The pages have been perforated to allow the evader to rip them out one by one. Designed together with Jonas Rimersma.

publication, 2023
